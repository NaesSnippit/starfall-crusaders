KNOWN DETAILS

Diamond, 9 years old. Blackjack hustler. Amateur sleight-of-hand expert. Nobody knows where she learned to count cards — but nobody wants to play against her twice.

She's small, fast-talking, and always carries a marked deck. Most assume she’s just a kid with shiny shoes… until they’re missing their wallet, pride, and lunch money.

COMET FESTIVAL BLACKJACK TABLE

In a shadowy alley behind the comet vendors, Diamond set up a “friendly” game of blackjack. A foldout table. Plastic chips. One tiny stool. No mercy.

Charles Couckee walked up with confidence and a crumpled $300 billfold. She offered him a seat. He took it. Worst decision of his life.

She dealt smooth, smiled wide, and never once drew a card she didn’t already know. Charles hit on 17. She stood on a 20 she’d built from thin air and spite.

“Better luck next year,” she said, sliding the chips into her backpack and vanishing into the fireworks crowd.

No one saw her again that night. The cards left behind weren’t hers. She switched the deck mid-hand.
